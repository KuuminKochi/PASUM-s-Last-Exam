Tutorial 2: Conditional Probability, Independent Events, and Bayes Theorem

Section A: Conditional Probability

Question 1 [10 marks]

Given: $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B|A) = 0.7$, $P(A' \cap B) = 0.3$

Part (a) [2 marks]

Find $P(A \cap B)$

Show solution

$P(B|A) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}$

$0.7 = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{0.4}$

$P(A \cap B) = 0.7 \times 0.4 = \boxed{0.28}$

Part (b) [2 marks]

Find $P(B)$

Show solution

$P(B) = P(A \cap B) + P(A' \cap B) = 0.28 + 0.3 = \boxed{0.58}$

Part (c) [2 marks]

Find $P(A \cup B)$

Show solution

$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) = 0.4 + 0.58 - 0.28 = \boxed{0.7}$

Part (d) [2 marks]

Find $P(A|B)$

Show solution

$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{0.28}{0.58} = \boxed{\frac{14}{29} \approx 0.483}$

Part (e) [2 marks]

Is $P(A|B) = P(B|A)$?

Show solution

$P(B|A) = 0.7$ (given)

$P(A|B) = \frac{14}{29} \approx 0.483$

No, they are not equal in general. $P(A|B) = P(B|A)$ only when $P(A) = P(B)$.

Question 2 [10 marks]

Survey of 1000 ex-students:

| | <4k | [4k,10k] | >10k | |---|---|---|---| | Class of 1990 | 250 | 300 | 80 | | Class of 1989 | 150 | 120 | 100 |

Part (a) [2 marks]

P(Class of 1990 AND earning < RM4000)

Show solution

$P = \frac{250}{1000} = \boxed{0.25}$

Part (b) [2 marks]

P(Earns > RM10000)

Show solution

Total >10k: 80 + 100 = 180

$P = \frac{180}{1000} = \boxed{0.18}$

Part (c) [3 marks]

P(Earns [4k,10k] | Class of 1989)

Show solution

Class of 1989 total: 150 + 120 + 100 = 370

$P = \frac{120}{370} = \boxed{\frac{12}{37} \approx 0.324}$

Part (d) [3 marks]

P(Class of 1990 | Earns > RM4000)

Show solution

Earns >4k: 300 + 80 + 120 + 100 = 600

Class 1990 AND >4k: 300 + 80 = 380

$P = \frac{380}{600} = \boxed{\frac{19}{30} \approx 0.633}$

Question 3 [6 marks]

Urn: 3 blue, 2 white balls. Choose 2 without replacement.

Part (a) [3 marks]

P(Second is blue | First is white)

Show solution

After removing 1 white: 3 blue, 1 white remain (4 total)

$P = \frac{3}{4} = \boxed{0.75}$

Part (b) [3 marks]

P(Both are blue)

Show solution

$P = \frac{3}{5} \times \frac{2}{4} = \frac{6}{20} = \boxed{0.3}$

Section B: Independent Events

Question 4 [9 marks]

P(Ameera gets A) = $\frac{3}{4}$, P(Bella gets A) = $\frac{1}{2}$, P(Celline gets A) = $\frac{4}{5}$

Events are independent.

Part (a) [3 marks]

P(All three get A)

Show solution

$P = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{5} = \frac{12}{40} = \boxed{\frac{3}{10} = 0.3}$

Part (b) [3 marks]

P(None gets A)

Show solution

$P = \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{5} = \boxed{\frac{1}{40} = 0.025}$

Part (c) [3 marks]

P(Only Ameera gets A)

Show solution

$P = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{5} = \boxed{\frac{3}{40} = 0.075}$

Section C: Bayes Theorem

Question 5 [12 marks]

Box A: 4 red, 2 white. Box B: 2 red, 2 white, 1 green.

Roll die: if < 3, choose from Box A; else choose from Box B.

Part (a) [3 marks]

P(Red from Box A)

Show solution

P(Box A) = $\frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}$

P(Red|A) = $\frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}$

$P = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{2}{3} = \boxed{\frac{2}{9}}$

Part (b) [3 marks]

P(White from Box B)

Show solution

P(Box B) = $\frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}$

P(White|B) = $\frac{2}{5}$

$P = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{2}{5} = \boxed{\frac{4}{15}}$

Part (c) [3 marks]

P(Red ball chosen)

Show solution

$P(Red) = P(Red|A)P(A) + P(Red|B)P(B)$

$= \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{9} + \frac{4}{15}$

$= \frac{10+12}{45} = \boxed{\frac{22}{45}}$

Part (d) [3 marks]

P(Box B | Red ball)

Show solution

$P(B|Red) = \frac{P(Red|B)P(B)}{P(Red)} = \frac{\frac{2}{5} \times \frac{2}{3}}{\frac{22}{45}}$

$= \frac{\frac{4}{15}}{\frac{22}{45}} = \frac{4}{15} \times \frac{45}{22} = \boxed{\frac{6}{11}}$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: This is Bayes' theorem - reverse conditional probability

Question 6 [10 marks]

College: 100 lecturers (70 full-time, 30 part-time) - Full-time: 45 drove car, 25 did not - Part-time: 12 drove car, 18 did not

Part (a) [8 marks]

Find:

##### (i) [2 marks] P(Part-time AND drove car)

Show solution

$P = \frac{12}{100} = \boxed{0.12}$

##### (ii) [2 marks] P(Full-time AND did not drive)

Show solution

$P = \frac{25}{100} = \boxed{0.25}$

##### (iii) [2 marks] P(Full-time OR drove car)

Show solution

$P = P(F) + P(D) - P(F \cap D) = \frac{70}{100} + \frac{57}{100} - \frac{45}{100}$

$= \frac{82}{100} = \boxed{0.82}$

##### (iv) [2 marks] P(Part-time | Drove car)

Show solution

Total drove car: 45 + 12 = 57

$P = \frac{12}{57} = \boxed{\frac{4}{19} \approx 0.211}$

Part (b) [2 marks]

Are "drove car" and "full-time" independent?

Show solution

Check: Does $P(Drove|Full-time) = P(Drove)$?

$P(Drove|Full-time) = \frac{45}{70} = \frac{9}{14} \approx 0.643$

$P(Drove) = \frac{57}{100} = 0.57$

No, they are not independent (0.643 ‚â† 0.57)

Question 7 [12 marks]

Computer company XX subcontracts: - 35% to A - 65% to B

Manufacturer A subcontracts: - 60% to C (1.5% defective) - 40% to D (1.0% defective)

Manufacturer B subcontracts: - 30% to E (1.2% defective) - 50% to F (1.25% defective) - 20% to G (0.85% defective)

Part (a) [6 marks]

P(Monitor is defective)

Show solution

$P(D) = P(D|C)P(C) + P(D|D)P(D) + P(D|E)P(E) + P(D|F)P(F) + P(D|G)P(G)$

$P(C) = 0.35 \times 0.60 = 0.21$

$P(D) = 0.35 \times 0.40 = 0.14$

$P(E) = 0.65 \times 0.30 = 0.195$

$P(F) = 0.65 \times 0.50 = 0.325$

$P(G) = 0.65 \times 0.20 = 0.13$

$P(D) = 0.015(0.21) + 0.01(0.14) + 0.012(0.195) + 0.0125(0.325) + 0.0085(0.13)$

$= 0.00315 + 0.0014 + 0.00234 + 0.0040625 + 0.001105$

$= \boxed{0.0120575 \approx 0.0121}$ or 1.21%

Part (b) [6 marks]

P(From C | Defective)

Show solution

$P(C|D) = \frac{P(D|C)P(C)}{P(D)} = \frac{0.015 \times 0.21}{0.0120575}$

$= \frac{0.00315}{0.0120575} = \boxed{0.261}$ or about 26.1%

Question 8 [10 marks]

Raqib drives to UM: - P(Route A) = $\frac{1}{3}$, P(Late|A) = $\frac{1}{5}$ - P(Route B) = $\frac{1}{5}$, P(Late|B) = $\frac{1}{4}$ - P(Route C) = $1 - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{7}{15}$, P(Late|C) = $\frac{3}{7}$

Part (a) [5 marks]

P(Late)

Show solution

$P(Late) = P(Late|A)P(A) + P(Late|B)P(B) + P(Late|C)P(C)$

$= \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{5} + \frac{3}{7} \times \frac{7}{15}$

$= \frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4+3+12}{60} = \boxed{\frac{19}{60} \approx 0.317}$

Part (b) [5 marks]

P(Route A | Late)

Show solution

$P(A|Late) = \frac{P(Late|A)P(A)}{P(Late)} = \frac{\frac{1}{15}}{\frac{19}{60}}$

$= \frac{1}{15} \times \frac{60}{19} = \boxed{\frac{4}{19} \approx 0.211}$

Question 9 [10 marks]

90 applicants for a job:

| | University Graduates | Non-graduates | |---|---|---| | ‚â•3 years experience | 18 | 9 | | <3 years experience | 36 | 27 |

A: University graduate, B: ‚â•3 years experience

Part (a) [6 marks]

Find:

##### (i) [2 marks] $P(A'|B')$

Show solution

$B'$ (less than 3 years): 36 + 27 = 63

$A' \cap B'$ (non-graduate AND <3 years): 27

$P(A'|B') = \frac{27}{63} = \boxed{\frac{3}{7}}$

##### (ii) [2 marks] $P(B|A)$

Show solution

University graduates: 18 + 36 = 54

Graduates with ‚â•3 years: 18

$P(B|A) = \frac{18}{54} = \boxed{\frac{1}{3}}$

##### (iii) [2 marks] $P(A \cup B)'$

Show solution

$(A \cup B)'$ means neither graduate nor ‚â•3 years experience

Non-graduates with <3 years: 27

$P = \frac{27}{90} = \boxed{0.3}$

Common Patterns Identified

Pattern 1: Bayes' Theorem Structure ‚Äî High frequency (Q5d, Q7b, Q8b)
Strategy: $P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}$ where $P(B)$ is total probability

Pattern 2: Independence Check ‚Äî Medium frequency (Q6b)
Strategy: Events independent iff $P(A|B) = P(A)$ or $P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B)$

Pattern 3: Tree Diagrams ‚Äî High frequency
Strategy: Draw tree for multi-stage problems to visualize conditional probabilities

Pattern 4: Law of Total Probability ‚Äî High frequency
Strategy: $P(B) = \sum P(B|A_i)P(A_i)$ for partition $A_i$

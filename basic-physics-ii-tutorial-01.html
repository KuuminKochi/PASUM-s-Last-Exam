Tutorial 1: Electrostatics

Question 1 [20 marks]

Three charges are placed along a straight line (Figure 1): - $q_1 = +35\mu$C - $q_2 = -10\mu$C - $q_3 = +60\mu$C

Distance $q_1$ to $q_2$: 20 cm Distance $q_2$ to $q_3$: 40 cm

Part (a) [10 marks]

Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force on $q_1$.

Show solution

Forces on q‚ÇÅ:

$q_1$ positive, $q_2$ negative ‚Üí $F_{12}$ is attractive ‚Üí to the right

$q_1$ positive, $q_3$ positive ‚Üí $F_{13}$ is repulsive ‚Üí to the left

$$F_{12} = \frac{k|q_1||q_2|}{r_{12}^2} = \frac{(9 \times 10^9)(35 \times 10^{-6})(10 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.20)^2}$$

$= \frac{3150 \times 10^{-3}}{0.04} = 7.88 \times 10^3$ N (to the right)

$$F_{13} = \frac{k|q_1||q_3|}{r_{13}^2} = \frac{(9 \times 10^9)(35 \times 10^{-6})(60 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.60)^2}$$

$= \frac{18900 \times 10^{-3}}{0.36} = 52.5$ N (to the left)

$F_{net} = 7880 - 52.5 = 7827.5$ N

Answer: Magnitude = $\boxed{7.83 \times 10^3}$ N, Direction = $\boxed{to\ the\ right}$ (toward $q_2$)

Part (b) [10 marks]

Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force on $q_2$.

Show solution

Forces on q‚ÇÇ:

$F_{21}$ from $q_1$: Attractive ‚Üí to the left = 7880 N

$F_{23}$ from $q_3$: Attractive ‚Üí to the right

$$F_{23} = \frac{(9 \times 10^9)(10 \times 10^{-6})(60 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.40)^2} = 3.38 \times 10^4$$ N

$F_{net} = 33750 - 7880 = 2.59 \times 10^4$ N (to the right)

Answer: Magnitude = $\boxed{2.59 \times 10^4}$ N, Direction = $\boxed{to\ the\ right}$ (toward $q_3$)

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: $q_2$ experiences larger force due to inverse square law ($F \propto 1/r^2$)

Question 2 [20 marks]

Three point charges in x-y plane (Figure 2): - $q_1 = -35\mu$C at origin - $q_2 = +10\mu$C at (0.5 m, 0) - $q_3 = +60\mu$C at 0.7 m, 30¬∞ above x-axis

Determine magnitude and direction of net electrostatic force on $q_2$.

Show solution

Position of $q_3$: $x_3 = 0.7\cos(30¬∞) = 0.606$ m, $y_3 = 0.7\sin(30¬∞) = 0.35$ m

Distance $q_2$ to $q_3$:

$\Delta x = 0.606 - 0.5 = 0.106$ m, $\Delta y = 0.35$ m

$r_{23} = \sqrt{(0.106)^2 + (0.35)^2} = 0.366$ m

Forces:

$F_{21} = \frac{(9 \times 10^9)(35 \times 10^{-6})(10 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.5)^2} = 12.6$ N (to the left, attractive)

$\vec{F}_{21} = (-12.6, 0)$

$F_{23} = \frac{(9 \times 10^9)(10 \times 10^{-6})(60 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.366)^2} = 40.3$ N (repulsive, away from $q_3$)

Angle: $\theta = 180¬∞ + \arctan(0.35/0.106) = 253¬∞$

$F_{23x} = 40.3\cos(253¬∞) = -11.8$ N, $F_{23y} = 40.3\sin(253¬∞) = -38.5$ N

Net: $F_x = -12.6 - 11.8 = -24.4$ N, $F_y = -38.5$ N

$F_{net} = \sqrt{(24.4)^2 + (38.5)^2} = 45.6$ N

$\theta = \arctan(38.5/24.4) = 57.6¬∞$ below negative x-axis

Answer: $\boxed{45.6}$ N at $\boxed{57.6¬∞}$ below negative x-axis (238¬∞ from +x)

Question 3 [15 marks]

Three charges (Figure 3): - $q_1 = +6.5$ nC at A - $q_2 = -3.0$ nC at B (15 cm from A) - $q_3 = +5.0$ nC at C (5 cm from B)

Point X is 30 cm from C (along the line). Find electric field at X.

Show solution

Positions: A at 0, B at 0.15 m, C at 0.20 m, X at 0.50 m

Distances from X: $r_1 = 0.50$ m, $r_2 = 0.35$ m, $r_3 = 0.30$ m

Fields:

$E_1 = \frac{k(6.5 \times 10^{-9})}{(0.50)^2} = 234$ N/C (‚Üí, away from $q_1$)

$E_2 = \frac{k(3.0 \times 10^{-9})}{(0.35)^2} = 220$ N/C (‚Üê, toward $q_2$)

$E_3 = \frac{k(5.0 \times 10^{-9})}{(0.30)^2} = 500$ N/C (‚Üí, away from $q_3$)

$E_{net} = 234 - 220 + 500 = 514$ N/C (to the right)

Answer: $\boxed{514}$ N/C to the $\boxed{right}$

Question 4 [20 marks]

Calculate electric field at point P (Figure 4): - $q_1 = +4$ nC at (0, 0.6 m) - $q_2 = +9.5$ nC at (1.8 m, 0.6 m) - $q_3 = -12$ nC at (0, 0) - P at (1.8 m, 0)

Show solution

Distances:

$r_1 = \sqrt{1.8^2 + 0.6^2} = 1.90$ m, $r_2 = 0.6$ m, $r_3 = 1.8$ m

Components:

$E_1 = 10$ N/C at $18.4¬∞$ below horizontal

$E_{1x} = 9.5$ N/C, $E_{1y} = -3.2$ N/C

$E_2 = 238$ N/C downward ($E_{2y} = -238$ N/C)

$E_3 = 33.3$ N/C to the left ($E_{3x} = -33.3$ N/C)

Net: $E_x = 9.5 - 33.3 = -23.8$ N/C, $E_y = -3.2 - 238 = -241$ N/C

$E_{net} = \sqrt{23.8^2 + 241^2} = 242$ N/C

$\theta = \arctan(241/23.8) = 84.4¬∞$ below negative x-axis

Answer: $\boxed{242}$ N/C at $\boxed{84.4¬∞}$ below negative x-axis

Question 5 [25 marks]

Electron: $v_0 = 1.9 \times 10^7$ m/s enters plates with $E = 4.5 \times 10^3$ N/C. Plate length: 66 mm, gap: 22 mm. Given: $q_e = -1.609 \times 10^{-19}$ C, $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg

Part (a) [5 marks]

Sketch the path.

Show solution

Parabolic path curving toward positive plate (upward if top plate positive).

Part (b) [5 marks]

Calculate acceleration.

Show solution

$a = \frac{|q|E}{m} = \frac{(1.609 \times 10^{-19})(4.5 \times 10^3)}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}$

$= \boxed{7.95 \times 10^{14}}$ m/s¬≤ toward positive plate

Part (c) [8 marks]

Horizontal and vertical velocity components on exit.

Show solution

Time: $t = \frac{0.066}{1.9 \times 10^7} = 3.47 \times 10^{-9}$ s

$v_x = v_0 = \boxed{1.9 \times 10^7}$ m/s (constant)

$v_y = at = (7.95 \times 10^{14})(3.47 \times 10^{-9}) = \boxed{2.76 \times 10^6}$ m/s

Part (d) [7 marks]

Angle of deflection.

Show solution

$\theta = \arctan(v_y/v_x) = \arctan(2.76/19) = \boxed{8.26¬∞}$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Electron deflects toward positive plate. Small angle due to high horizontal speed.

Common Patterns

Pattern 1: Like charges repel, opposite charges attract. Use vector addition.

Pattern 2: Electric field: positive ‚Üí points away; negative ‚Üí points toward.

Pattern 3: Electron in E-field: $a = qE/m$, parabolic trajectory (like projectile).

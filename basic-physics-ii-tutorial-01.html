Question 1 [6 marks]

Figure 1 shows three charges $q_1$, $q_2$, and $q_3$ placed along a straight line. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force acting on: - (a) $q_1$ - (b) $q_2$

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.2, >=stealth] % Charges \filldraw[red] (0,0) circle (3pt) node[above] {$q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C}$}; \filldraw[blue] (2,0) circle (3pt) node[above] {$q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C}$}; \filldraw[red] (6,0) circle (3pt) node[above] {$q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C}$};

% Distance labels \draw[<->] (0,-0.5) -- (2,-0.5) node[midway,below] {$20\,$cm}; \draw[<->] (2,-0.5) -- (6,-0.5) node[midway,below] {$40\,$cm};

% Force arrows on q1 \draw[->, thick, green!60!black] (0,0) -- (-1.5,0) node[above] {$F_{12}$} node[midway,above] {$\leftarrow$}; \draw[->, thick, red!60!black] (0,0) -- (-2,0) node[above] {$F_{13}$} node[midway,above] {$\leftarrow$};

% Force arrows on q2 \draw[->, thick, green!60!black] (2,0) -- (3.5,0) node[above] {$F_{21}$} node[midway,above] {$\rightarrow$}; \draw[->, thick, blue!60!black] (2,0) -- (4.5,0) node[above] {$F_{23}$} node[midway,above] {$\rightarrow$};

% Axis \draw[->] (-0.5,0) -- (7,0) node[right] {+x}; \end{tikzpicture}

Need a hint?

Use Coulomb's Law: $F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$. Remember forces are vectors - you need to consider direction. Like charges repel, opposite charges attract. For $q_1$, it feels repulsion from $q_2$ (push left) and from $q_3$ (push left).

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C} = 35 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C} = 60 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $r_{12} = 20\,$cm $= 0.20\,$m, $r_{23} = 40\,$cm $= 0.40\,$m - $r_{13} = 60\,$cm $= 0.60\,$m

(a) Force on $q_1$:

Forces on $q_1$: - From $q_2$ (repulsive, to the left): $F_{12} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(10\times10^{-6})}{(0.2)^2} = 78.75\,$N (left) - From $q_3$ (repulsive, to the left): $F_{13} = k\frac{q_1 q_3}{r_{13}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.6)^2} = 52.5\,$N (left)

$$F_{\text{net on }q_1} = 78.75 + 52.5 = 131.25\,$N (toward negative x direction / left) (b) Force on $q_2$:

Forces on $q_2$: - From $q_1$ (repulsive, to the right): $F_{21} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = 78.75\,$N (right) - From $q_3$ (repulsive, to the right): $F_{23} = k\frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(10\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.4)^2} = 33.75\,$N (right)

$$F_{\text{net on }q_2} = 78.75 + 33.75 = 112.5\,$N (toward positive x direction / right)

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Coulomb's law gives the MAGNITUDE of the force. The direction depends on whether charges attract (opposite) or repel (like). Like charges push apart, opposite charges pull together. The force on $q_1$ from $q_2$ pushes $q_1$ away from $q_2$, i.e., toward negative x.

Question 2 [8 marks]

Figure 2 shows three point charges, $q_1$, $q_2$, and $q_3$ located in the x-y plane. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force acting on $q_2$.

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.3, >=stealth] % Axes \draw[->] (-0.5,0) -- (3,0) node[right] {$x$}; \draw[->] (0,-0.5) -- (0,2.5) node[above] {$y$};

% q1 at origin \filldraw[red] (0,0) circle (3pt) node[below left] {$q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C}$};

% q2 at (0.5, 0) \filldraw[blue] (2,0) circle (3pt) node[below] {$q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C}$};

% q3 above q2 at angle \filldraw[red] (2,1.4) circle (3pt) node[right] {$q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C}\,(300^\circ)$};

% Distance labels \draw[<->] (0,-0.3) -- (2,-0.3) node[midway,below] {$0.5\,$m}; \draw[<->] (2,0.1) -- (2,1.3) node[midway,right] {$0.7\,$m};

% Triangle outline \draw[dashed] (0,0) -- (2,1.4);

% Force on q2 from q1 (repulsive, to the left) \draw[->, thick, red!60!black] (2,0) -- (0.5,0) node[midway,above] {$F_{21}$};

% Force on q2 from q3 (repulsive, direction away from q3) \draw[->, thick, red!60!black] (2,0) -- (2,-1) node[midway,right] {$F_{23}$};

% 30 degree label \draw[dashed] (2,0) -- (2.7,0.5); \node at (2.5,0.3) {$30^\circ$}; \end{tikzpicture}

Need a hint?

This is a 2D problem. Break forces into x and y components. $F_{21}$ acts along the x-axis (left). $F_{23}$ acts along the line from $q_2$ to $q_3$ - since they're like charges, $q_2$ is repelled away from $q_3$. Use trigonometry to find components.

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C}$ at origin - $q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C}$ at $(0.5, 0)$ m - $q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C}$ at $(0.5\cos300^\circ, 0.5\sin300^\circ)$ relative to $q_2$... wait, let me recalculate

From the diagram: - Distance $q_1$ to $q_2$: $r_{12} = 0.5\,$m - Distance $q_2$ to $q_3$: $r_{23} = 0.7\,$m - $q_3$ is at $30^\circ$ above the negative x-axis from $q_2$ (or $330^\circ$)

Force on $q_2$ from $q_1$ (along negative x): $$F_{21} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(10\times10^{-6})}{(0.5)^2} = 12.6\,$N$$

Direction: negative x-axis (left)

Force on $q_2$ from $q_3$ (repulsive, along line away from $q_3$): $$F_{23} = k\frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(10\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.7)^2} = 11.02\,$N$$

Direction: $30^\circ$ below negative x-axis (since $q_3$ is above $q_2$)

Components of $F_{23}$: $$F_{23x} = 11.02 \cos(30^\circ + 180^\circ) = 11.02 \cos(210^\circ) = -9.55\,$N $$F_{23y} = 11.02 \sin(30^\circ + 180^\circ) = 11.02 \sin(210^\circ) = -5.51\,$N Total force on $q_2$: $$F_x = F_{21x} + F_{23x} = -12.6 + (-9.55) = -22.15\,$N $$F_y = F_{21y} + F_{23y} = 0 + (-5.51) = -5.51\,$N $$F_{\text{net}} = \sqrt{22.15^2 + 5.51^2} = 22.83\,$N Direction: $$\tan\theta = \frac{|F_y|}{|F_x|} = \frac{5.51}{22.15} = 0.249$$ $$\theta = 14.0^\circ$$

The force points $14.0^\circ$ below the negative x-axis (or $194.0^\circ$ from +x).

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: In 2D force problems, always draw a clear diagram showing all charges and their positions. The force on a charge is along the line connecting the two charges - repulsive for like charges (away from the other charge), attractive for unlike charges (toward the other charge). Use vector components!

Question 3 [8 marks]

Figure 3 shows three isolated point charges, $q_1$, $q_2$ and $q_3$, separated by distances AB and BC. Determine the magnitude and direction of the electric field strength at point X.

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.2, >=stealth] % Charges on x-axis \filldraw[red] (0,0) circle (3pt) node[below] {$q_1 = +6.5\,$nC at A}; \filldraw[blue] (3,0) circle (3pt) node[below] {$q_2 = -3.0\,$nC at B}; \filldraw[red] (4,0) circle (3pt) node[below] {$q_3 = +5.0\,$nC at C};

% Point X above \filldraw[black] (0,1.5) circle (2pt) node[above] {$X$};

% Vertical dashed lines \draw[dashed] (0,0) -- (0,1.5); \draw[dashed] (3,0) -- (0,1.5); \draw[dashed] (4,0) -- (0,1.5);

% Distance labels on x-axis \draw[<->] (0,-0.4) -- (3,-0.4) node[midway,below] {$15\,$cm}; \draw[<->] (3,-0.4) -- (4,-0.4) node[midway,below] {$5\,$cm};

% Distances from charges to X \draw[<->] (0,0.1) -- (0,1.4) node[midway,left] {$15\,$cm}; \draw[<->] (3,0.1) -- (0.3,1.4) node[midway,right] {$r_{2X}$}; \draw[<->] (4,0.1) -- (0.1,1.4) node[midway,right] {$r_{3X}$};

% Electric field arrows (pointing away from positive, toward negative) \draw[->, thick, red!60!black] (0,1.5) -- (0,0.3) node[midway,left] {$E_1$}; \draw[->, thick, blue!60!black] (0,1.5) -- (0.8,1.5) node[midway,above] {$E_2$}; \draw[->, thick, red!60!black] (0,1.5) -- (0.2,0.9) node[midway,right] {$E_3$};

% x-axis \draw[->] (-0.5,0) -- (5,0) node[right] {$x$}; \end{tikzpicture}

Need a hint?

Electric field $E = k\frac{Q}{r^2}$ points AWAY from positive charges and TOWARD negative charges. At point X, calculate each field contribution separately (magnitude and direction), then add as vectors. Watch units: convert cm to m, nC to C ($1\,$nC $= 10^{-9}\,$C).

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +6.5\,$nC at A (positive, x = 0) - $q_2 = -3.0\,$nC at B (negative, x = 15 cm) - $q_3 = +5.0\,$nC at C (positive, x = 20 cm) - Point X is 15 cm above point A (vertically aligned with A)

Distances from X to each charge:

- $r_{1X} = 15\,$cm $= 0.15\,$m - $r_{2X} = \sqrt{(15)^2 + (15)^2} = 15\sqrt{2}\,$cm $= 0.212\,$m - $r_{3X} = \sqrt{(20)^2 + (15)^2} = 25\,$cm $= 0.25\,$m

Electric field from each charge:

$E_1$ (from $q_1$, away from positive, downward):

$$E_1 = \frac{k q_1}{r_{1X}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(6.5\times10^{-9})}{(0.15)^2} = 2600\,$N/C (down)

$E_2$ (toward $q_2$, since negative, to the right):

$$E_2 = \frac{k |q_2|}{r_{2X}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(3.0\times10^{-9})}{(0.212)^2} = 600\,$N/C (right, $45^\circ$ below horizontal)

$E_3$ (away from $q_3$, positive, to the right and down):

$$E_3 = \frac{k q_3}{r_{3X}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(5.0\times10^{-9})}{(0.25)^2} = 720\,$N/C

Angle of $E_3$: $\tan\theta = \frac{15}{20} = 0.75$, so $\theta = 36.9^\circ$ below horizontal

Components:

$$E_{3x} = 720\cos(36.9^\circ) = 576\,$N/C $$E_{3y} = -720\sin(36.9^\circ) = -432\,$N/C Total field at X: $$E_x = E_{2x} + E_{3x} = 600 + 576 = 1176\,$N/C $$E_y = E_{1y} + E_{2y} + E_{3y} = -2600 + 0 + (-432) = -3032\,$N/C $$E = \sqrt{1176^2 + 3032^2} = 3239\,$N/C Direction: $$\tan\theta = \frac{|E_y|}{E_x} = \frac{3032}{1176} = 2.58$$ $$\theta = 68.8^\circ$$

The field points $68.8^\circ$ below the positive x-axis (mostly downward).

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Electric field is a vector field. The direction is the direction of the FORCE that would act on a positive test charge. Positive source charges push positive test charges away (field points away from source). Negative source charges pull positive test charges toward them (field points toward source).

Question 4 [8 marks]

Based on Figure 4, calculate the magnitude and direction of electric field strength at point P.

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.1, >=stealth] % Coordinate axes \draw[->] (-0.5,0) -- (5,0) node[right] {$x$}; \draw[->] (0,-0.5) -- (0,3) node[above] {$y$};

% q1 at (0.6, 0.6) \filldraw[red] (1.2,1.2) circle (3pt) node[above left] {$q_1 = +4\,$nC};

% q2 at (1.8, 1.8) \filldraw[red] (3.6,3.6) circle (3pt) node[above] {$q_2 = +9.5\,$nC};

% q3 at lower left \filldraw[blue] (1.2,0) circle (3pt) node[below] {$q_3 = -12\,$nC};

% Point P \filldraw[black] (4,0) circle (2pt) node[below right] {$P$};

% Distance annotations \draw[<->] (1.2,0.3) -- (4,0.3) node[midway,above] {$2.8\,$m}; \draw[<->] (3.6,3.0) -- (4,3.0) node[midway,above] {$0.4\,$m}; \draw[<->] (1.2,-0.3) -- (4,-0.3) node[midway,below] {$2.8\,$m};

% Field direction indicators (qualitative) % E from q1 pushes away from q1 toward P direction % E from q2 pushes away from q2 toward P % E from q3 pulls toward q3 (left and up) \end{tikzpicture}

Need a hint?

Calculate each electric field contribution at point P: $E = kQ/r^2$. Watch the geometry - $q_1$ is at a diagonal to P, $q_2$ is almost directly above P, and $q_3$ is to the left. Add the vector components carefully.

Show solution Given (from diagram):

- $q_1 = +4\,$nC at position such that distance to P $= 2.8\,$m - $q_2 = +9.5\,$nC at distance $0.4\,$m from P - $q_3 = -12\,$nC at distance $2.8\,$m from P

Electric field contributions at P:

$E_1$ (from $q_1$, away from positive):

$$E_1 = \frac{k q_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(4\times10^{-9})}{(2.8)^2} = 4.59\,$N/C

Direction: From $q_1$ toward P, approximately horizontal (to the right)

$E_2$ (from $q_2$, away from positive):

$$E_2 = \frac{k q_2}{r_2^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(9.5\times10^{-9})}{(0.4)^2} = 534.4\,$N/C

Direction: From $q_2$ toward P, downward (vertically)

$E_3$ (toward $q_3$, since negative):

$$E_3 = \frac{k |q_3|}{r_3^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(12\times10^{-9})}{(2.8)^2} = 13.8\,$N/C

Direction: Toward $q_3$, which is to the left of P (so field points left)

Components (taking positive x to right, positive y up): $$E_{1x} = 4.59\,$N/C (right), $E_{1y} = 0$$ $$E_{2x} = 0, E_{2y} = -534.4\,$N/C (down)$$ $$E_{3x} = -13.8\,$N/C (left), E_{3y} = 0$$ Total: $$E_x = 4.59 + 0 - 13.8 = -9.21\,$N/C $$E_y = 0 - 534.4 + 0 = -534.4\,$N/C $$E = \sqrt{9.21^2 + 534.4^2} = 534.5\,$N/C Direction: $$\tan\theta = \frac{534.4}{9.21} = 58.0$$ $$\theta = 89.0^\circ$$

The field points almost straight down (negative y direction).

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: The $q_2$ charge dominates because it's much closer to point P (0.4 m vs 2.8 m). Since $E \propto 1/r^2$, the nearest charge has the biggest effect. Even though $q_3$ has the largest magnitude (-12 nC), being 7 times farther away makes its contribution much smaller.

Question 5 [10 marks]

An electron traveling at speed of $1.9 \times 10^7 \, \text{ms}^{-1}$ enters the region between two parallel metal plates. The uniform electric field strength between the plates is $4.5 \times 10^3 \, \text{NC}^{-1}$.

(Given: $q_e = 1.609 \times 10^{-19}\,$C, $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,$kg)

- (a) Sketch the path of electron as it travels between the parallel plates - (b) Calculate the acceleration of electron between the plates due to uniform electric field - (c) Determine the horizontal and vertical components of electron's velocity as it emerges from the region between the plates - (d) Calculate the angle of deflection of the electron beam as it exits the plates

\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.3, >=stealth] % Parallel plates \draw[thick] (0,0) -- (6,0) node[right] {$+$} node[midway,above] {$22\,$mm}; \draw[thick] (0,-1.5) -- (6,-1.5) node[right] {$-$} node[midway,below] {$66\,$mm};

% Electric field arrows (downward, from + to -) \foreach \x in {1,2,3,4,5} { \draw[->, blue!60!black] (\x, -0.2) -- (\x, -1.3); } \node[blue] at (6.5, -0.75) {$E = 4.5 \times 10^3\,$N/C};

% Electron path (parabolic, curved downward since negative charge feels force opposite to E) \draw[->, red, very thick] (0, -0.5) -- (1.5, -0.5) node[midway,above] {$v_x$}; \draw[red, very thick, domain=0:6] plot (\x, {-0.5 - 0.08\x\x}) node[right] {electron path};

% Initial velocity label \draw[->, green!60!black] (0.5, -0.8) -- (1.5, -0.8) node[midway,below] {$v_0 = 1.9 \times 10^7\,$m/s};

% Deflection angle \draw[dashed] (6, -0.5) -- (7, -0.5); \draw[dashed] (6, -0.5) -- (7, -1.1); \draw[red] (6.3, -0.5) -- (6.5, -0.65) -- (6.7, -0.5); \node at (7.2, -0.8) {$\theta$};

% Dimensions \draw[<->] (6.2,0) -- (6.2,-1.5) node[midway,right] {$d = 6.6\,$cm}; \draw[<->] (0,0.3) -- (6,0.3) node[midway,above] {$L = 6.6\,$cm};

% Labels \node[above] at (0,0.3) {entry}; \node[above] at (6,0.3) {exit}; \end{tikzpicture}

Need a hint?

For (b): Use $F = ma = qE$. Since electron has negative charge, acceleration is opposite to the field direction. For (c): Horizontal velocity stays constant (no horizontal force). Vertical velocity increases: $v_y = at$. For (d): $\tan\theta = v_y/v_x$.

Show solution Given:

- Electron initial speed: $v_0 = 1.9 \times 10^7\,$m/s (enters horizontally) - Electric field: $E = 4.5 \times 10^3\,$N/C (from + to - plate) - Electron charge: $q_e = -1.609 \times 10^{-19}\,$C - Electron mass: $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,$kg - Plate length: $L = 6.6\,$cm $= 0.066\,$m - Plate separation: $d = 6.6\,$cm (but for uniform field, assume electron stays well within)

(a) Path sketch:

The electron curves downward (toward the positive plate) because: - Force on negative charge: $F = qE = (-e)(E)$, direction OPPOSITE to E-field - E-field points from + to - plate - So force on electron points from - to + plate (upward in diagram, which is actually downward relative to initial direction)

Actually wait - looking at the diagram: + plate is at top, - plate at bottom. E points DOWN. Electron (negative) feels force UP. So the electron curves toward the positive (top) plate.

(b) Acceleration: $$F = qE = (-1.609 \times 10^{-19})(4.5 \times 10^3) = -7.24 \times 10^{-16}\,$N$$

Direction: upward (opposite to E-field, toward + plate)

$$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{-7.24 \times 10^{-16}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}} = -7.95 \times 10^{14}\,$m/s¬≤

The negative sign indicates acceleration upward (toward + plate).

(c) Velocity components at exit: Horizontal component (constant, no horizontal force): $$v_x = v_0 = 1.9 \times 10^7\,$m/s$$ Vertical component:

Time in field region: $t = \frac{L}{v_x} = \frac{0.066}{1.9 \times 10^7} = 3.47 \times 10^{-9}\,$s

$$v_y = a \cdot t = (7.95 \times 10^{14})(3.47 \times 10^{-9}) = 2.76 \times 10^6\,$m/s$$ (d) Deflection angle: $$\tan\theta = \frac{v_y}{v_x} = \frac{2.76 \times 10^6}{1.9 \times 10^7} = 0.145$$ $$\theta = 8.3^\circ$$

The electron beam deflects by $8.3^\circ$ toward the positive plate.

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: This is essentially projectile motion under constant electric force (like a horizontal projectile in gravity). Key points:

1. The electron's path is PARABOLIC (like a projectile under gravity) 2. Negative charge accelerates opposite to E-field direction 3. Horizontal velocity is unchanged (no horizontal force) 4. The deflection angle is small because the electron is very light (small mass), so it accelerates rapidly but doesn't have much time in the field

Common Patterns

Coulomb's Law:

- $F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$ - $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2/\text{C}^2$ - Like charges: repel (push apart) - Unlike charges: attract (pull together)

Electric Field:

- $E = \frac{F}{q_{\text{test}}} = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$ - Direction: away from positive, toward negative - Superposition: vector sum of all contributions

Electron in Electric Field:

- $F = qE = ma$ (Newton's 2nd law) - For negative charge: acceleration is opposite to E-field - Projectile motion: parabolic path - $v_x$ constant, $v_y = at$

Problem-Solving Strategy:

1. Draw clear diagram showing positions of all charges 2. For each charge pair, determine force direction (attract/repel) 3. Break forces/fields into x and y components 4. Add vectorially (watch signs!) 5. Calculate magnitude and direction of result

Unit Conversions:

- $1\,\mu\text{C} = 10^{-6}\,$C - $1\,$nC $= 10^{-9}\,$C - $1\,$cm $= 0.01\,$m

Tutorial 1: Electrostatics - Coulomb's Law & Electric Field

Question 1 [6 marks]

Figure 1 shows three charges $q_1$, $q_2$, and $q_3$ placed along a straight line. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force acting on: - (a) $q_1$ - (b) $q_2$

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

Need a hint?

Use Coulomb's Law: $F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$. Remember forces are vectors - you need to consider direction. Like charges repel, opposite charges attract. For $q_1$, it feels repulsion from $q_2$ (push left) and from $q_3$ (push left).

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C} = 35 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C} = 60 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}$ - $r_{12} = 20\,$cm $= 0.20\,$m, $r_{23} = 40\,$cm $= 0.40\,$m - $r_{13} = 60\,$cm $= 0.60\,$m

(a) Force on $q_1$:

Forces on $q_1$: - From $q_2$ (repulsive, to the left): $F_{12} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(10\times10^{-6})}{(0.2)^2} = 78.75\,$N (left) - From $q_3$ (repulsive, to the left): $F_{13} = k\frac{q_1 q_3}{r_{13}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.6)^2} = 52.5\,$N (left)

$$F_{\text{net on }q_1} = 78.75 + 52.5 = 131.25\,$N (toward negative x direction / left) (b) Force on $q_2$:

Forces on $q_2$: - From $q_1$ (repulsive, to the right): $F_{21} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = 78.75\,$N (right) - From $q_3$ (repulsive, to the right): $F_{23} = k\frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(10\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.4)^2} = 33.75\,$N (right)

$$F_{\text{net on }q_2} = 78.75 + 33.75 = 112.5\,$N (toward positive x direction / right)

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Coulomb's law gives the MAGNITUDE of the force. The direction depends on whether charges attract (opposite) or repel (like). Like charges push apart, opposite charges pull together. The force on $q_1$ from $q_2$ pushes $q_1$ away from $q_2$, i.e., toward negative x.

Question 2 [8 marks]

Figure 2 shows three point charges, $q_1$, $q_2$, and $q_3$ located in the x-y plane. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electrostatic force acting on $q_2$.

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

Need a hint?

This is a 2D problem. Break forces into x and y components. $F_{21}$ acts along the x-axis (left). $F_{23}$ acts along the line from $q_2$ to $q_3$ - since they're like charges, $q_2$ is repelled away from $q_3$. Use trigonometry to find components.

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +35\,\mu\text{C}$ at origin - $q_2 = +10\,\mu\text{C}$ at $(0.5, 0)$ m - $q_3 = +60\,\mu\text{C}$ at $30^\circ$ above the negative x-axis from $q_2$

Force on $q_2$ from $q_1$ (along negative x): $$F_{21} = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(35\times10^{-6})(10\times10^{-6})}{(0.5)^2} = 12.6\,$N (left) Force on $q_2$ from $q_3$: $$F_{23} = k\frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}^2} = \frac{(9\times10^9)(10\times10^{-6})(60\times10^{-6})}{(0.7)^2} = 11.02\,$N

Direction: $30^\circ$ below negative x-axis

Components of $F_{23}$: $$F_{23x} = 11.02 \cos(210^\circ) = -9.55\,$N $$F_{23y} = 11.02 \sin(210^\circ) = -5.51\,$N Total force on $q_2$: $$F_x = -12.6 + (-9.55) = -22.15\,$N $$F_y = -5.51\,$N $$F_{\text{net}} = \sqrt{22.15^2 + 5.51^2} = 22.83\,$N Direction: $\theta = 14.0^\circ$ below the negative x-axis

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: In 2D force problems, always draw a clear diagram. The force on a charge is along the line connecting the two charges - repulsive for like charges, attractive for unlike charges.

Question 3 [8 marks]

Figure 3 shows three isolated point charges at positions A, B, C. Determine the magnitude and direction of the electric field strength at point X.

Given: $k = 9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{Nm}^2\text{C}^{-2}$

Need a hint?

Electric field $E = k\frac{Q}{r^2}$ points AWAY from positive charges and TOWARD negative charges. At point X, calculate each field contribution separately, then add as vectors.

Show solution Given:

- $q_1 = +6.5\,$nC at A (x = 0) - $q_2 = -3.0\,$nC at B (x = 15 cm) - $q_3 = +5.0\,$nC at C (x = 20 cm) - Point X is 15 cm above point A

Distances from X:

- $r_{1X} = 15\,$cm $= 0.15\,$m - $r_{2X} = \sqrt{15^2 + 15^2} = 0.212\,$m - $r_{3X} = \sqrt{20^2 + 15^2} = 0.25\,$m

Electric field contributions:

$E_1$ (from $q_1$, away, downward): $E_1 = 2600\,$N/C

$E_2$ (toward $q_2$, right): $E_2 = 600\,$N/C at $45^\circ$ below horizontal

$E_3$ (away from $q_3$): $E_3 = 720\,$N/C at $36.9^\circ$ below horizontal

Total: $$E_x = 1176\,$N/C, \quad E_y = -3032\,$N/C$$ $$E = 3239\,$N/C at $68.8^\circ$ below +x

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Electric field direction is the direction of the FORCE on a positive test charge. Positive charges push away, negative charges pull toward.

Question 5 [10 marks]

An electron traveling at $1.9 \times 10^7 \, \text{m/s}$ enters parallel plates. The electric field is $4.5 \times 10^3 \, \text{N/C}$. Calculate: - (a) Sketch the path - (b) Acceleration - (c) Velocity components at exit - (d) Deflection angle

Need a hint?

For (b): Use $F = ma = qE$. Since electron has negative charge, acceleration is opposite to the field direction (toward + plate). For (c): Horizontal velocity stays constant, vertical velocity increases: $v_y = at$. For (d): $\tan\theta = v_y/v_x$.

Show solution Given:

- $v_0 = 1.9 \times 10^7\,$m/s - $E = 4.5 \times 10^3\,$N/C (from + to -) - $q_e = -1.609 \times 10^{-19}\,$C - $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,$kg

(a) Path: Electron curves toward the + plate (upward) because negative charge feels force opposite to E-field. (b) Acceleration: $$F = qE = (-1.609 \times 10^{-19})(4.5 \times 10^3) = -7.24 \times 10^{-16}\,$N $$a = \frac{F}{m} = -7.95 \times 10^{14}\,$m/s¬≤ (upward) (c) At exit: $$v_x = 1.9 \times 10^7\,$m/s (constant) $$t = \frac{L}{v_x} = 3.47 \times 10^{-9}\,$s $$v_y = a \cdot t = 2.76 \times 10^6\,$m/s$$ (d) Deflection angle: $$\tan\theta = \frac{2.76 \times 10^6}{1.9 \times 10^7} = 0.145$$ $$\theta = 8.3^\circ$$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: This is projectile motion under constant electric force. Key: negative charge accelerates opposite to E-field direction.

Common Patterns

Coulomb's Law:

- $F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$ - Like charges: repel; Unlike: attract

Electric Field:

- $E = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$ - Direction: away from positive, toward negative

Electron in Electric Field:

- $F = qE = ma$ - For negative charge: acceleration opposite to E-field

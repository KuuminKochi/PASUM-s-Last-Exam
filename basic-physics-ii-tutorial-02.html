Tutorial 2: Electrostatics - Gauss's Law

Question 1 [2 marks]

State Gauss's Law and write the Gauss Law equation.

Need a hint?

Gauss's Law relates the electric flux through a closed surface to the enclosed charge.

Show solution Gauss's Law: The net electric flux through any closed Gaussian surface is proportional to the net charge enclosed. Equation: $$\Phi_E = \oint \mathbf{E} \cdot d\mathbf{A} = \frac{Q_{\text{enclosed}}}{\varepsilon_0}$$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Gauss's Law is most useful for surfaces with high symmetry (spherical, cylindrical, or planar).

Question 2 [5 marks]

Electric charge is distributed uniformly along a long charged wire; the charge per unit length is $\lambda$. Find the electric field at distance $r$ from the wire.

Need a hint?

Use a cylindrical Gaussian surface coaxial with the wire. The field is radially outward and constant on the curved surface.

Show solution

For an infinitely long wire with linear charge density $\lambda$:

Using cylindrical Gaussian surface of radius r and length L: Flux through curved surface: $\Phi_E = E(2\pi r L)$ Enclosed charge: $Q = \lambda L$ Gauss's Law: $E(2\pi r L) = \frac{\lambda L}{\varepsilon_0}$ $$E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi r \varepsilon_0}$$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: The field decreases as $1/r$ (not $1/r^2$) because the "effective area" grows with circumference.

Question 3 [5 marks]

Two parallel plates with area 2.0 m¬≤ each carry ¬±5.0 ŒºC. Find: - (i) Surface charge density - (ii) Electric field between plates

Need a hint?

Surface charge density $\sigma = Q/A$. Between parallel plates, $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$.

Show solution (i) Surface charge density: $$\sigma = \frac{Q}{A} = \frac{5.0 \times 10^{-6}}{2.0} = 2.5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{C/m}^2$$ (ii) Electric field: $$E = \frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_0} = \frac{2.5 \times 10^{-6}}{8.85 \times 10^{-12}} = 2.82 \times 10^5 \, \text{N/C}$$

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Between plates: $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$. Outside: $E = 0$ (fields cancel).

Extra Question [6 marks]

Electric field 3.50 kN/C along x-axis. Find flux through 0.350m √ó 0.700m plane: - (a) parallel to yz plane - (b) parallel to xy plane - (c) normal at 40¬∞ to x-axis

Need a hint?

Flux $\Phi_E = EA\cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is angle between field and normal.

Show solution

Given: $E = 3500\,$N/C, $A = 0.350 \times 0.700 = 0.245\,$m¬≤

(a) Plane || yz: $\theta = 0¬∞$, $\Phi_E = 857.5\,$Nm¬≤/C (b) Plane || xy: $\theta = 90¬∞$, $\Phi_E = 0$ (c) Normal at 40¬∞: $\theta = 40¬∞$, $\Phi_E = 656.9\,$Nm¬≤/C

‚ö†Ô∏è Nuance: Flux positive when field lines leave surface, negative when they enter.

Common Patterns

Gauss's Law Applications:

- Spherical: $E = kQ/r^2$ - Cylindrical: $E = \lambda/(2\pi\varepsilon_0 r)$ - Planar: $E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$

Electric Flux:

- $\Phi_E = EA\cos\theta$

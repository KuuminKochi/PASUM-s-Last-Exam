Section A: Counting and Permutation

Question 1 [5 marks]

Without using a calculator, evaluate $\frac{_nP_r}{_nP_{r-1}}$.

Need a hint?

Recall that $_nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$. Write out both permutations and simplify.

Show solution

Method: Using permutation formula

$$\frac{_nP_r}{_nP_{r-1}} = \frac{\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}}{\frac{n!}{(n-r+1)!}} = \frac{(n-r+1)!}{(n-r)!} = \boxed{n-r+1}$$

Question 2 [6 marks]

In a pet competition, in how many ways can the organiser arrange 3 dogs and 5 cats in a row such that the dogs must be separated?

Need a hint?

First arrange the 5 cats, then place the 3 dogs in the gaps between cats.

Show solution

Method: Gap method

Step 1: Arrange 5 cats in $5!$ ways

Step 2: This creates 6 gaps: _ C _ C _ C _ C _ C _

Step 3: Choose 3 gaps from 6: $_6P_3 = 6 \times 5 \times 4$

Total: $5! \times 6 \times 5 \times 4 = \boxed{14400}$

Question 3 Part (a) [3 marks]

Find the number of ways to arrange the ten letters of MOTIVATION with no restriction.

Show solution

MOTIVATION has 10 letters with T repeated twice.

Ways: $\frac{10!}{2!} = \boxed{1814400}$

Section B: Combination

Question 1 Part (a) [3 marks]

Find $n$ if $_nC_6 = {_nC_3}$.

Show solution

Property: If $_nC_r = {_nC_s}$ and $r \neq s$, then $r + s = n$

$n = 6 + 3 = \boxed{9}$

Question 1 Part (b) [3 marks]

Find $n$ if $_nC_2 = 28$.

Show solution

$\frac{n(n-1)}{2} = 28$

$n(n-1) = 56$

$n^2 - n - 56 = 0$

$(n-8)(n+7) = 0$

$n = \boxed{8}$

Question 2 Part (a) [3 marks]

A team of 5 from 12 boys and 9 girls. How many ways if 3 boys and 2 girls?

Show solution

$_{12}C_3 \times {_9C_2} = 220 \times 36 = \boxed{7920}$

Section C: Probability

Question 1 Part (a) [2 marks]

A number from {1, 2, 3, ..., 250}. Probability it's divisible by 5?

Show solution

Numbers divisible by 5: $\frac{250}{5} = 50$

$P = \frac{50}{250} = \boxed{\frac{1}{5}}$

Question 1 Part (c) [2 marks]

Probability divisible by 3 or 4?

Show solution

$P(3) = \frac{83}{250}$, $P(4) = \frac{62}{250}$, $P(12) = \frac{20}{250}$

$P(3 \cup 4) = \frac{83 + 62 - 20}{250} = \boxed{\frac{1}{2}}$

Tutorial 1: Chemical Equilibrium

Overview

This tutorial covers chemical equilibrium concepts including equilibrium constants ($K_c$ and $K_p$), reaction quotient, and equilibrium calculations.

Topics: Equilibrium constants, $K_c$, $K_p$, Reaction Quotient Difficulty: Medium Estimated Time: 90 minutes

Question 1 [20 marks]

Part (a) [15 marks]

For each of the following reactions: (i) Write expression for $K_c$ and $K_p$ (ii) Write the unit of the equilibrium constants $K_c$ and $K_p$ if concentrations are measured in mol dm‚Åª¬≥ and partial pressures in atm.

I. [3 marks]

$$4A(g) + B(g) \rightleftharpoons 2C(g) + 2D(g)$$
Show solution

(i) Expressions:

$$K_c = \frac{[C]^2[D]^2}{[A]^4[B]}$$

$$K_p = \frac{(P_C)^2(P_D)^2}{(P_A)^4(P_B)}$$

(ii) Units:

$K_c$: $\frac{(\text{mol dm}^{-3})^4}{(\text{mol dm}^{-3})^5} = \text{mol}^{-1} \text{dm}^3$

$K_p$: $\frac{\text{atm}^4}{\text{atm}^5} = \text{atm}^{-1}$

II. [3 marks]

$$E(g) + F(s) \rightleftharpoons G(g) + H(g)$$
Show solution

Note: Solids are not included in equilibrium expressions

$$K_c = \frac{[G][H]}{[E]}$$

$$K_p = \frac{P_G \cdot P_H}{P_E}$$

Units: $K_c$: mol dm‚Åª¬≥, $K_p$: atm

III. [3 marks]

$$2J(g) + L(g) \rightleftharpoons M(g) + 2N(s)$$
Show solution

$$K_c = \frac{[M]}{[J]^2[L]}$$

$$K_p = \frac{P_M}{(P_J)^2 \cdot P_L}$$

Units: $K_c$: mol‚Åª¬≤ dm‚Å∂, $K_p$: atm‚Åª¬≤

IV. [3 marks]

$$Fe(s) + 5CO(g) \rightleftharpoons Fe(CO)_5(g)$$
Show solution

$$K_c = \frac{[Fe(CO)_5]}{[CO]^5}$$

$$K_p = \frac{P_{Fe(CO)_5}}{(P_{CO})^5}$$

Units: $K_c$: mol‚Åª‚Å¥ dm¬π¬≤, $K_p$: atm‚Åª‚Å¥

V. [3 marks]

$$Cu^{2+}(aq) + 4NH_3(aq) \rightleftharpoons Cu(NH_3)_4^{2+}(aq)$$
Show solution

$$K_c = \frac{[Cu(NH_3)_4^{2+}]}{[Cu^{2+}][NH_3]^4}$$

No $K_p$ - aqueous solutions, not gases

Unit: $K_c$: mol‚Åª‚Å¥ dm¬π¬≤

Part (b) [5 marks]

Consider: $N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NH_3(g)$ with $K_c = 3.7 \times 10^8$ mol‚Åª¬≤ L¬≤ at 25¬∞C

Determine $K_c$ for:

(i) [2 marks]

$$2NH_3(g) \rightleftharpoons N_2(g) + 3H_2(g)$$
Show solution

Reverse reaction: $K_c' = \frac{1}{K_c} = \frac{1}{3.7 \times 10^8} = \boxed{2.7 \times 10^{-9}}$ mol¬≤ L‚Åª¬≤

(ii) [2 marks]

$$\frac{1}{2}N_2(g) + \frac{3}{2}H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons NH_3(g)$$
Show solution

Halved coefficients: $K_c' = (K_c)^{1/2} = (3.7 \times 10^8)^{1/2} = \boxed{1.9 \times 10^4}$ mol‚Åª¬π L

(iii) [1 mark]

$$NH_3(g) \rightleftharpoons \frac{1}{2}N_2(g) + \frac{3}{2}H_2(g)$$
Show solution

Reverse of (ii): $K_c' = \frac{1}{1.9 \times 10^4} = \boxed{5.3 \times 10^{-5}}$ mol L‚Åª¬π

Question 2 [16 marks]

Part (a) [4 marks]

Carbon dioxide reacts with carbon: $C(s) + CO_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2CO(g)$

At 700¬∞C, a 2.5 L flask contains 0.10 mol CO, 0.25 mol CO‚ÇÇ and 0.40 mol C. Calculate $K_c$.

Show solution

Step 1: Calculate concentrations

$[CO] = \frac{0.10}{2.5} = 0.04$ M

$[CO_2] = \frac{0.25}{2.5} = 0.10$ M

Step 2: Write $K_c$ expression (exclude solid C)

$$K_c = \frac{[CO]^2}{[CO_2]} = \frac{(0.04)^2}{0.10} = \frac{0.0016}{0.10} = \boxed{0.016}$$

Part (b) [12 marks]

$H_2(g) + I_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2HI(g)$

Equilibrium mixture: 0.2 mol H‚ÇÇ and 0.4 mol I‚ÇÇ in 2.0 L. Given $K_c = 1.5 \times 10^{-6}$.

(i) [2 marks]

Is the system at homogeneous or heterogeneous equilibrium?

Show solution

All species are gases ‚Üí Homogeneous equilibrium

(ii) [4 marks]

Calculate the equilibrium concentration of HI.

Show solution

$[H_2] = \frac{0.2}{2.0} = 0.1$ M, $[I_2] = \frac{0.4}{2.0} = 0.2$ M

$$K_c = \frac{[HI]^2}{[H_2][I_2]}$$

$$1.5 \times 10^{-6} = \frac{[HI]^2}{(0.1)(0.2)}$$

$[HI]^2 = 3.0 \times 10^{-8}$

$[HI] = \boxed{1.73 \times 10^{-4}}$ M

(iii) [3 marks]

Predict the position of equilibrium.

Show solution

$K_c = 1.5 \times 10^{-6} \ll 1$

Equilibrium lies far to the left (reactants favored)

(iv) [3 marks]

Determine $K_c$ for the reverse reaction.

Show solution

$K_c' = \frac{1}{K_c} = \frac{1}{1.5 \times 10^{-6}} = \boxed{6.67 \times 10^5}$

Question 3 [14 marks]

Part (a) [6 marks]

$S_2Cl_4(g) \rightleftharpoons 2SCl_2(g)$

3.0 moles of S‚ÇÇCl‚ÇÑ in 1.5 L vessel. At equilibrium, 0.5 moles of S‚ÇÇCl‚ÇÑ remain. Calculate $K_c$.

Show solution

ICE Table:

| | S‚ÇÇCl‚ÇÑ | SCl‚ÇÇ |

|---|-------|------|

|I | 3.0 | 0 |

|C | -2.5 | +5.0 |

|E | 0.5 | 5.0 |

Concentrations: $[S_2Cl_4] = \frac{0.5}{1.5} = 0.333$ M

$[SCl_2] = \frac{5.0}{1.5} = 3.333$ M

$$K_c = \frac{[SCl_2]^2}{[S_2Cl_4]} = \frac{(3.333)^2}{0.333} = \boxed{33.3}$$

Part (b) [8 marks]

$PCl_5(g) \rightleftharpoons PCl_3(g) + Cl_2(g)$

5.0 mol PCl‚ÇÖ in 2.5 dm¬≥ at T¬∞C. $K_c = 0.02$ mol dm‚Åª¬≥.

(i) [6 marks]

Calculate equilibrium concentrations of all species.

Show solution

Initial $[PCl_5] = \frac{5.0}{2.5} = 2.0$ M

Let degree of dissociation = $\alpha$

At equilibrium: $[PCl_5] = 2(1-\alpha)$, $[PCl_3] = 2\alpha$, $[Cl_2] = 2\alpha$

$$K_c = \frac{(2\alpha)(2\alpha)}{2(1-\alpha)} = \frac{4\alpha^2}{2(1-\alpha)} = 0.02$$

Solving: $\alpha = 0.095$

$[PCl_5] = 2(0.905) = \boxed{1.81}$ M

$[PCl_3] = [Cl_2] = 2(0.095) = \boxed{0.19}$ M

(ii) [2 marks]

Find the degree of dissociation, $\alpha$.

Show solution

$\alpha = \boxed{0.095}$ or $\boxed{9.5\%}$

Question 4 [8 marks]

Mixture: 4.0 mol NO‚ÇÇ, 2.0 mol NO, 1.5 mol O‚ÇÇ in 2.0 dm¬≥ at 300¬∞C.

$2NO_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NO(g) + O_2(g)$, $\Delta H = -120$ kJ, $K_c = 0.168$ mol dm‚Åª¬≥

Find $Q_c$ and predict reaction direction.

Show solution

Concentrations: $[NO_2] = 2.0$, $[NO] = 1.0$, $[O_2] = 0.75$ M

$$Q_c = \frac{[NO]^2[O_2]}{[NO_2]^2} = \frac{(1.0)^2(0.75)}{(2.0)^2} = \frac{0.75}{4} = 0.1875$$

$Q_c = 0.188 > K_c = 0.168$

Since $Q_c > K_c$, reaction shifts to the left (toward reactants)

Question 5 [10 marks]

$H_2(g) + Cl_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2HCl(g)$

20.0 g Cl‚ÇÇ heated with 1.0 g H‚ÇÇ at 470¬∞C. At equilibrium, 14.0 g Cl‚ÇÇ remain.

Calculate $K_c$ and predict equilibrium position. (RAM: H = 1.0, Cl = 35.5)

Show solution

Molar masses: H‚ÇÇ = 2, Cl‚ÇÇ = 71, HCl = 36.5

Initial moles: H‚ÇÇ = 0.5, Cl‚ÇÇ = 0.282

Cl‚ÇÇ reacted = 20.0 - 14.0 = 6.0 g = $\frac{6.0}{71} = 0.0845$ mol

From stoichiometry:

H‚ÇÇ reacted = 0.0845 mol, HCl formed = 0.169 mol

Equilibrium moles: H‚ÇÇ = 0.4155, Cl‚ÇÇ = 0.1975, HCl = 0.169

(Assuming 1 L volume for $K_c$ calculation)

$$K_c = \frac{[HCl]^2}{[H_2][Cl_2]} = \frac{(0.169)^2}{(0.4155)(0.1975)} = \boxed{0.348}$$

$K_c < 1$ ‚Üí Equilibrium lies slightly to the left

Question 6 [18 marks]

$2HI(g) \rightleftharpoons H_2(g) + I_2(g)$, $\Delta H = -53$ kJ mol‚Åª¬π

3.0 mol HI in 2.0 L at 500¬∞C. At equilibrium, 60% of HI dissociated.

Part (i) [6 marks]

How many moles of each gas at equilibrium?

Show solution

HI dissociated = 60% of 3.0 = 1.8 mol

From equation: 2HI ‚Üí H‚ÇÇ + I‚ÇÇ

H‚ÇÇ formed = I‚ÇÇ formed = $\frac{1.8}{2} = 0.9$ mol

HI remaining = 3.0 - 1.8 = 1.2 mol

Answer: HI = 1.2 mol, H‚ÇÇ = 0.9 mol, I‚ÇÇ = 0.9 mol

Part (ii) [6 marks]

Calculate $K_c$ and $K_p$ at 500¬∞C. (R = 0.0821 L atm K‚Åª¬π mol‚Åª¬π)

Show solution

Concentrations: $[HI] = 0.6$, $[H_2] = [I_2] = 0.45$ M

$$K_c = \frac{(0.45)(0.45)}{(0.6)^2} = \frac{0.2025}{0.36} = \boxed{0.5625}$$

$K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$ where $\Delta n = 0$

$K_p = K_c = \boxed{0.5625}$ (or 0.56 to 2 s.f.)

Part (iii) [6 marks]

What happens to equilibrium position and $K_c$ when:

(I) [3 marks]

Temperature is increased?

Show solution

$\Delta H = -53$ kJ (exothermic)

Increasing T: Equilibrium shifts to left (toward reactants)

$K_c$ decreases

(II) [3 marks]

More HI is added?

Show solution

Adding HI (reactant): Equilibrium shifts to right (toward products)

$K_c$ unchanged (constant at constant T)

Question 7 [14 marks]

$N_2O_4(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NO_2(g)$ at 25¬∞C

Initial $P_{N_2O_4} = 2.5$ atm. At equilibrium, $P_{N_2O_4} = 1.75$ atm.

Part (i) [8 marks]

Calculate $K_p$.

Show solution

$N_2O_4$ dissociated: $2.5 - 1.75 = 0.75$ atm

$NO_2$ formed: $2 \times 0.75 = 1.5$ atm

Total pressure at equilibrium: $1.75 + 1.5 = 3.25$ atm

$$K_p = \frac{(P_{NO_2})^2}{P_{N_2O_4}} = \frac{(1.5)^2}{1.75} = \frac{2.25}{1.75} = \boxed{1.29}$$ atm

Part (ii) [6 marks]

Calculate $K_c$ at same temperature. (R = 0.0821 L atm K‚Åª¬π mol‚Åª¬π)

Show solution

$K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$ where $\Delta n = 2 - 1 = 1$

$1.29 = K_c(0.0821 \times 298)^1$

$K_c = \frac{1.29}{24.47} = \boxed{0.0527}$ mol L‚Åª¬π
